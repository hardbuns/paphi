Welcome to Alpha Chapter



Pi Alpha Phi

UC Berkeley

Pi Alpha Phi is the first Asian-American interest fraternity founded in 1929 by six young men looking to create an organization based on academic excellence, asian awareness, brotherhood, leadership, and philanthropy. For over 80 years strong, Pi Alpha Phi has been helping like-minded individuals realize these goals and develop into leaders in their communities. The Alpha Chapter (first chapter) was established at the University of California Berkeley and the fraternity has expanded to nineteen other campuses on the east and west coast.

About

Eighty Four Years Strong

Pi Alpha Phi was founded in 1929 at UC Berkeley. The origin of Pi Alpha Phi traces back to the six founding fathers: D. Wing Tom, Wing Chan, Elmer Leong, Chack Chan, Tim Jang, and George Lee. These men created the organization on the qualities of:

  1. Academic Excellence
  2. Asian Awareness
  3. Brotherhood
  4. Leadership
  5. Philanthropy

The fraternity has expanded 13 chapters thriving on the east and west coast. Through shared experience and friendship, we create what is essential to any fraternity - brotherhood. With brotherhood as an immutable asset, the lifelong bonds that is developed extend far beyond the university setting.

Our chapter here at UC Berkeley holds brotherhood events with other chapters and exchanges with sororities at UC Berkeley and other campuses. This provides excellent networking opputunities as well as potential lasting friendships.
